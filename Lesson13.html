Lesson 13: Inference from Two Independent Samples

Lesson Introduction

Lesson Outcomes

Conduct a Hypothesis Test for the true difference of two means \(\mu_1-\mu_2\) from independent samples:

  • State the null and alternative hypothesis.
  • Show the appropriate numerical and graphical summaries that support the hypothesis test.
  • Check the requirements for the hypothesis test.
  • Calculate the test-statistic, degrees of freedom, and \(p\)-value of the hypothesis test.
  • Assess the statistical significance by comparing the \(p\)-value to the significance level, \(\alpha\).
  • Draw a correct conclusion for the hypothesis test.

Calculate and interpret a Confidence Interval for the true difference of two means \(\mu_1-\mu_2\) from independent samples:

  • Show the appropriate numerical and graphical summaries that support the confidence interval.
  • Check the requirements for the confidence interval.
  • Calculate the confidence interval for a given level of confidence.
  • Identify the point estimate and margin of error for the confidence interval.
  • Interpret the confidence interval for a given level of confidence.
